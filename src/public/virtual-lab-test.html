Stoichiometry Guide

Basic Steps

  1. Balance the chemical equation
  2. Convert given mass/volume to moles
  3. Use molar ratios to find moles of product
  4. Convert moles to requested unit

Example Calculation

Problem:

HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H2O

Given: 100 mL of 0.5 M HCl and 100 mL of 0.25 M NaOH

  1. Calculate moles of HCl:
    0.5 M × 0.1 L = 0.05 moles
  2. Calculate moles of NaOH:
    0.25 M × 0.1 L = 0.025 moles
  3. Determine limiting reagent:
    NaOH (0.025 moles) is limiting
  4. Calculate product:
    0.025 moles NaCl will form

Key Formulas

Molarity (M) = moles/liters
Moles = Molarity × Volume (L)
Moles = Mass/Molar Mass
